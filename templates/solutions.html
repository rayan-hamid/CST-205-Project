Solutions

Cut Back on Energy at Home

We rely a lot on coal, oil, and gas for electricity, but you can help reduce that impact by lowering your heating or cooling, switching to energy-saving bulbs, and opting for energy-efficient appliances. Little changes like washing your clothes in cold water or air-drying them can make a huge difference!

Ditch the Car When You Can

Cars are a big source of pollution. Walk, bike, or take public transport instead. Not only is it better for the planet, but it's also great for your health. If you need to travel long distances, trains or buses are a solid option, and carpooling is always a smart move.

More Veggies, Less Meat

Eating more plant-based foods can drastically cut down on your carbon footprint. Plant-based meals use fewer resources to produce and release fewer greenhouse gases. So, next time you're meal prepping, throw in some extra veggies!

Travel Smarter

Air travel uses up a lot of fossil fuels, so think twice before booking a flight. If possible, consider taking a train or carpooling. Or better yet, meet virtually! If you do need to travel, maybe save flights for the really important trips.

Cut Down on Food Waste

Throwing food away wastes the resources and energy used to produce it. Plus, when food rots, it creates harmful gases. To reduce waste, try planning your meals, eating leftovers, and composting whatever’s left.

Reduce, Reuse, Repair & Recycle

Everything we buy—from electronics to clothes—creates carbon emissions. Instead of buying brand new all the time, try fixing or reusing things. And remember to recycle when you can. Every little bit helps.

Go Green with Your Energy

If your home energy comes from non-renewable sources like oil or coal, consider switching to solar or wind power. Check if your utility company offers renewable energy plans and make the change for a cleaner, greener future.

Switch to an Electric Vehicle

If you’re in the market for a new car, why not go electric? Electric vehicles reduce pollution and are much better for the environment compared to traditional gas-powered cars. And now, they’re more affordable than ever with serval options!
